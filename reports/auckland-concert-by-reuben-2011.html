Auckland Concert Report 2011 by Reuben
Added: 7 May, 2011
Concert: Auckland, Vector Arena - May 6th, 2011
Written by: Webmaster Reuben - that's me!

After a year and a half of waiting to see my second ever live André concert, the waiting was well worth it. After travelling up from Tauranga to meet with Jenny on Friday morning, we headed to the meet and greet at the Copthorne and had a lovely afternoon with other fans who from the website here.

Then off to the concert at 7:30pm! After waiting in our seats, 8pm quickly came about and the famous Seventy Six Trombones began to play – then just as they hopped up on stage, I quickly managed to snap a photo on my phone and upload it to the website Facebook page! André and his orchestra played many of the popular Strauss pieces, including Vienna Blood, and the Skater Waltz, -- where a marvellous sight of ‘snow’ fell down from the arena ceiling just four rows behind us!! 

Then, of course, the wonderful Platin Tenors came out and serenaded us with songs such as ‘Mama’. Then before we knew it, balloons had begun to fall and The Seekers came out and joined André on stage! They sounded just like their original recordings. Fantastic. They looked like they were having a great time up on stage, they’ve still got it! It was great to see how the audience really got involved this year! You could see many people waltzing in the aisles during The Blue Danube, - including ourselves. Then near the end of the concert, all the people standing up at the front of the stage, dancing and singing, -it was truly a wonderful sight. I cannot imagine anyone leaving the show disappointed. Another night I am sure to not forget!
