Maastricht Concert Report by Kiwi Barbara
Added: 03 August, 2010
Concert: Vrijthof, Maastricht, July, 2010
Written by: Barbara Henderson
Photos: A few photos on this page. Also, check out AR Movies for videos of the Maastricht concerts including an interview with Barbabas' father, Bill.

After 12 months of planning on Thursday 8th July we arrived by train in Maastricht. Arriving at the hotel we meet the first of the many Andr� fans from around the world who descend on Maastricht at this time.

Europe was in the grips of a heat wave at this time. While last year visitors were out trying to buy thermals, this year everyone was out buying fans and trying to find somewhere cool. Anywhere that had air conditioning was very popular.
Late in the afternoon from our hotel room we watched and listened as Andr� and the orchestra, choir and soloists went through the sound check. It was funny to watch Andr� riding around on a seqway (not sure this is the right word) checking the sound from all parts of the square and making sure everyone would have perfect acoustics. During this time the crowd in the area of the square slowly built up and around 9.30pm the gates were opened and there was a made dash to get the best seats to watch the dress rehearsal. Again this was fun as Andr� tried different presentations for the items to be performed. We now had a good idea what the program would be and this only increased the excitement for the concert the next evening. The dress rehearsal finally finished about midnight and we went to bed excited about the first concert the next night.

Friday night rolled around and after advice from various fans we meet about 8.00pm we went to the front of the Theatre in preparation for the concert. This is where the performers get ready for the concert and is an ideal place to meet them. The first person we meet was Carmen (soloist) and what a delight she is. She was more than willing to talk to us and became very animated when I asked her when we could expect her CD. She explained that with her schedule she was having trouble completing it, but that hopefully it will not be too much longer. She then posed with us for a photo before going to get ready. We then saw various other orchestra members stranding around chatting, eating ice cream, smoking and just relaxing. Everyone we approached was more than willing to stop and talk to us and have photos taken. We spoke to Agnes (violin) and Nadia (viola) about New Zealand and they were very complimentary about their visit last year and are looking forward to coming back in October. During this time Dad was interviewed by the local TV station, at first he had no idea what was going on but managed to give a good interview. This can be seen on Ineke and Ruuds website. Then it was off to find our seats. While waiting for the concert to begin a local band marches around the square entertaining the crowds. When they finished the opening bars of 76 Trombones began and Andr� and the Orchestra were entering the square and moving onto the stage. The excitement in the audience was palpable with everyone clapping and swaying, a great night had begun.

Not speaking Dutch we were at a disadvantage not understanding everything that was being said, but we could get the idea of what was going on.

I am sure you have all seen details of the concert itself so will not go into that other than to give our impression. The concert was up to Andr�s high standard and the Carmen, Mirusia and Kimmi (the Black diamond from South Africa) were amazing as always. The Platin Tenors were there usual brilliant best, and I always think it would be nice to hear more from them. In the first half the highlight for me was the African children and the fun they had which spilled over to the audience. They were greeted with tremendous applause and it seems Andr� has again uncovered some potential performers. During the second half Andr� highlighted two of our favourite orchestra members in Tanya (cellist) and Manoe (clarinet, bagpipes and anything else you can think of). This showcase of their talents was exceptional and highlighted what talent there is within the orchestra. The concert finished with the usual array of encores, and the Limburg anthem and a local Maastricht song, which got all the locals involved and it was great to be in the middle of the audience. By the end of the evening we were more than pleased with the concert and so happy we had decided to make the long journey.

The next day was the Fan dinner on the riverboat. This was attended d by 73 people from 12 countries. We were lucky enough to be the first New Zealanders to attend. Gerd Leers the former mayor of Maastricht did the formal welcome and we were lucky that Gary Bennett also attended for a time. This was a great time to meet many of the people we had been messaging on the various websites. We had a lovely buffet meal on a lovely setting, the Maas river. Ineke and Ruud did a wonderful job organizing this and everyone had a great time getting to know each other. The dinner finished in time for those attending that nights concert to get there in plenty of time.

We did not attend that nights concert but watched it from our room. The surprise was the appearance of DJ �tzi, who had not been at the concert the previous night. He is a great entertainer who had the whole audience singing along to Sweet Caroline. It was a great sight watching the people in the cafes joining in and interacting during the concert.
As Holland had made the World Cup soccer final and that was due to start at the same time as the concert, the concert was moved to 1.00pm. As we were expecting another really hot day they moved the concert to the MECC, an indoor concert venue. This gave us another perspective to the concerts. The walls of the concert hall had facades put up to make it look like the cafes on the square, and there were 93 chandeliers hung. When we went into the hall all the chairs had orange hats and fans on them. When we opened the box with the fans we found that the fans we had were blue and on looking around saw that others had orange ones, so being a bit cheeky (not at all like me) I went to some empty seats and when I found orange ones swapped them for ours. Sorry to the people who got the blue ones. It was another polished and impressive concert with both the Berlin Harmonists and DJ �tzi.

When we got back to the square we found it had been transformed into a soccer arena and they were expecting 10,000 people to watch the soccer final. And so we joined what turned out to be closer to 20,000 fans to cheer on Holland. What a shame they were disappointed!

The concert on the Tuesday was again slightly different in that after the fireworks had gone off at the end of the concert there was a fire in the rafters of the stage. Andr� and the Orchestra were very professional and completed the concert before vacating the stage so they could put the fire out. I am sure many people did not even realise what had occurred.

Our final concert was the Friday night and I am ashamed to say we left at the interval. Just as we were making our way to our seats the heavens opened and there was a heavy downfall of rain. We were absolutely drenched, even though Andr� had put out ponchos it was to late by the time we got to our seats. As we had to leave early the next morning for London at the interval we decided it was getting cold and so we went back to our room got dry and watched the rest from the warmth and dryness of our room. This was unfortunate as it was Dads 86th birthday and was meant to be a treat for him. But as it turned out this concert was a highlight for Dad as he meet and had his photo taken with Manoe. Manoe was the one member of the orchestra he had wanted to meet right from the time we decided to go to Maastricht.

The next morning we left Maastricht and returned to London before journeying back to NZ.

So was flying for 23hours and then travelling by train and bus to get to Maastricht worth it? YES YES YES those 9 days we were in Maastricht were just wonderful. From meeting all the fans from around the world, to meeting the members of the orchestra, choir and the soloists, to the concerts themselves we had a amazing time. This was a trip we will never forget and although I realize not everyone can afford to do it, if you get the chance grab it as you will not regret it.

My thanks must go to Ineke and Ruud for organizing our wonderful room and the fan dinner and for keeping us informed about the changes to the Sunday concert. Also to all the people we meet who were so friendly and welcoming, you made us feel very special. I am sorry we did not have more chance to speak to you all but we did enjoy meeting everyone for however brief a time. And finally to Andr� and the Orchestra, Choir and Soloists thank you for amazing concerts that we will remember forever. And yes we do hope that one day we will get to return.
